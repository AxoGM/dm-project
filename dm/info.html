Information

Coronavirus symptoms can vary widely, ranging from mild to severe. Generally, COVID-19 symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure and include:

1. Common Symptoms:

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headache
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea

2. Severe Symptoms (medical attention needed):

  • Difficulty breathing
  • Persistent chest pain or pressure
  • Confusion or inability to stay awake
  • Bluish lips or face

Long COVID is a condition where some people experience lingering symptoms for weeks or months after the initial infection. Common long COVID symptoms include fatigue, shortness of breath, brain fog, and joint or muscle pain.
